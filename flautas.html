Flautas!

  • Written by Alejandro Chang                                 
  • Food                                 

Flautas are easy to make, easy to clean and is a scalable dinner that can be done for 5 people or 50 people pretty easily. Flautas definitely taste best when fresh so try to calculate how many you will need ahead of time.

The ingedient list is as follows:

• Tortillas (flour or corn) *I prefer corn
• Chicken
• 4 Potatoes
• 2 cup of mozarella cheese
• 2 cups of shreded lettuce
• Sour Cream
• Fresh cheese (queso fresco) or dried cheese
• Wooden Toothpicks

The biggest part of this cooking process is the prepwork and the assembly. Once your prep/assembly is done however, it is pretty easy and repetitive process. You can begin the process by boiling your potatoes until soft. Or boiling your chicken, seasoning it to your liking and shredding it. Something that works pretty well is if you buy a rotisserie chicken from a place like Costco, shred it and then you're done.

Once meat/potatos are ready, you can assembled your toppings.The assembly process consists of the sour cream, shredde lettuce, fresh or dry cheese and a hot sauce of your liking, tapatio taste great if you have it. Once thats done, the flauta making process can begin. You can begin by mixing your chicken and potatoes, add the 2 cups of cheese (or up to your liking) and continue mixing until it looks like the below picture. Note: It can just be chicken, chicken + potatoes or potatoes + cheese if you prefer not to use meat.


Once thats done, begin by heating a pan with oil. Grab your tortillas and wrap them in the filling(chicken/potato/cheese). Place a toothpick through the wrapped flautas and place them in the frying pan. The flautas should look like this below.


Make sure the frying pan has enough oil so the flautas can fry on all sides. Once each flauta begins to look cripsy feel free to take the tooth pick out. Once they start looking like the picture below they are close to finish.


At this point the flautas are done. All they need is the toppings. I would recomend adding lettuce, sour cream and a little bit of fresh cheese. Absolutely delicious. Let me know how your Flautas turn out to be!
